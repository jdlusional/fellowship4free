Welcome to Issue 001

I am Jonathan Lin Davis. I have received multiple fellowships throughout graduate school, and I have come to believe that finding money should not cost you money.

Fellowships4Free is one of several public transparency projects I am building across the different corners of my life and work. The goal in each case is the same: to share the guidance I have received, or wish I had, with people trying to navigate the same terrain. This one is about funding your graduate education without paying to find out what is available.

The full index lives at fellowship4free.com. It has 50 verified fellowships right now, and it updates weekly: new cycles are added as they open, expired ones are removed as they close. This newsletter follows the same rhythm. Every Tuesday I will surface what is closing soon, spotlight one program worth a closer look, and flag anything urgent.

If this is useful to you, forward it or share the link. For a shorter weekly snapshot, follow the Fellowships4Free LinkedIn newsletter, which runs an abbreviated version of each issue.

◆ Spotlight fellowship

Paul and Daisy Soros Fellowships  ·  Open Field

Paul and Daisy Soros Fellowship for New Americans

Opens

Spring 2026

Deadline

Oct 29, 2026

Funding

Up to $90,000

One of the most generous and distinctive fellowships in American graduate education. Up to $90,000 across two years covers both stipend and tuition at any graduate or professional program in any field. The application closes October 29, 2026 at 2 PM ET.

Designed for immigrants and children of immigrants, eligibility requires you to be a New American: a naturalized US citizen, green card holder, asylee, refugee, DACA recipient, or a US-born citizen whose parents were not both born as US citizens. You must also be 30 or younger and not past your third year of graduate study.

⚠  New Americans only  ·  Age 30 or younger  ·  Not past 3rd year of grad program

◆ Five worth knowing

All five have open or opening cycles. Deadlines range from this fall through early 2027.

National Science Foundation  ·  STEM and Engineering

NSF Graduate Research Fellowship Program (GRFP)

Deadline

Oct–Nov 2026

Funding

$37,000/yr + $16,000 tuition; 3 yrs

The most portable STEM fellowship in graduate education. Three years of funding, transferable between institutions, with no service requirement. Deadlines vary by discipline, typically mid-October through mid-November. Open to US citizens, nationals, and permanent residents in STEM and STEM education fields.

Knight-Hennessy Scholars, Stanford  ·  Open Field

Knight-Hennessy Scholars Program

Deadline

Oct 6, 2026

Funding

Full funding up to 3 years

The world's largest fully endowed university-wide graduate fellowship, open to applicants from every country in any field. Full tuition, living stipend, annual travel, and relocation support at Stanford. Requires concurrent admission to a Stanford graduate program. Roughly 100 scholars selected each year.

Hertz Foundation  ·  STEM and Engineering

Hertz Fellowship

Opens

Late Aug 2026

Deadline

Late Oct 2026

Funding

Up to $250,000 over 5 years

Among the most prestigious STEM fellowships available, the Hertz combines a ~$44,000 nine-month stipend with full tuition and a network of exceptional applied scientists and engineers. Open to college seniors, first-year graduate students, and gap-year applicants pursuing a PhD in applied physical or biological sciences, math, or engineering at a US institution. US citizens and permanent residents only.

National Academy of Education  ·  Education Research

NAEd/Spencer Dissertation Fellowship

Deadline

~Oct 2, 2026

Funding

$27,500

One of the most respected dissertation fellowships in education research. Thirty-five fellowships awarded annually to doctoral candidates whose work focuses on education in any discipline, from any US institution. US citizenship is not required and undocumented students are explicitly eligible.

Horizon Public Service  ·  Public Policy and Government

Horizon Public Service Fellowship

Deadline

~Sep 2, 2026

Funding

$75,000–$113,000 + benefits

A full-time, salaried public service fellowship in Washington DC for recent master's and doctoral graduates. Fellows receive $75,000 (junior) or $113,000 (senior) plus $15,000 for health, $3,000 for professional development, and $3,000 relocation. Green card holders are eligible. This is the closest deadline in the index as of July 1.
